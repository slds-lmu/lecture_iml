\aufgabe{PFI: Comparing Linear Regression and Gradient Boosting}{
We compare PFI on synthetic data with six features on linear regression vs. gradient boosting.
Both models are fit on the same data (train-test split: 65\% vs. 35\%) with performance below.

\begin{minipage}{0.55\textwidth}
	\textbf{Data generating process:}
$$ y = 3x_1 +  1.5\,x_3^2 + 2\sin(2\,x_4) + \varepsilon, \varepsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0,0.7^2)$$
	\[
	\begin{aligned}
		x_1,x_3,x_4,x_5,x_6 &\sim \mathcal{N}(0,1),	\\
		x_2 = x_1 + \varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_2 &\sim \mathcal{N}(0,0.25^2)
	\end{aligned}
	\]
\end{minipage}
\hfill
\begin{minipage}{0.44\textwidth}
	\begin{tabular}{lcc}
		\toprule
		Model & Train MSE & Test MSE \\
		\midrule
		Lin. Reg. & 6.22 & 7.13 \\
		Boosting & 0.0007 & 1.46 \\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
\end{minipage}

\begin{minipage}{0.44\textwidth}
	Feature roles: 
	\begin{itemize}[nosep]
		\item $x_1$ has a strong linear effect
		\item $x_2$ is correlated (redundant)
		\item $x_3$ and $x_4$ have a nonlinear effect
		\item $x_5$ and $x_6$ are noise
	\end{itemize}
\end{minipage}
\hfill

	\begin{enumerate}
		\item For the lin. reg. model, identify the two most important features and explain why $x_1$ appears dominant.
		\item Compare the PFI rankings between the two models. Which features gain importance when moving from linear regression to gradient boosting?
		\item Which features remain negligible (near zero) in both models and why?
	\end{enumerate}

	\item \textbf{Understanding model-specific differences.}
	\begin{enumerate}
		\item Explain why the importance of $x_4$ and $x_3$ differs between the two models. Why are they underestimated in the linear model?
		\item Why can $x_2$ show a small non-zero importance in the linear model?
	\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}
